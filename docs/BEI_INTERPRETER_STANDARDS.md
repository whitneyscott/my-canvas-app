# BEI interpreter standards (derived from Texas HHS Handbook)

**Source:** [Chapter 1 — General Interpreter Certification Policies and Procedures](https://www.hhs.texas.gov/handbooks/board-evaluation-interpreters/chapter-1-general-interpreter-certification-policies-procedures) (Texas Health and Human Services, Board for Evaluation of Interpreters). Content reflects revision notices through **Nov. 10, 2025** where cited on that page.

This document **reorganizes** handbook material into **standard groups**. It is not a substitute for the official handbook, statutes, or TAC; use it for product alignment, curriculum mapping, or quick reference only.

---

## 1. Certification eligibility & prerequisites

**Baseline for any BEI examination or certificate**

- Be at least **18** years old.
- Hold a **high school diploma or equivalent**.
- Have **no disqualifying criminal conviction** (as defined by rules on denial, probation, suspension, revocation, or other discipline).

**Test of English Proficiency (TEP)**

- Meet baseline requirements above.
- Complete at least **30 semester credit hours** from an accredited college or university, **cumulative GPA ≥ 2.0**.

**Performance test (general interpreter levels)**

- Meet baseline requirements.
- **Pass the TEP** (unless applying only for a **specialty** certificate).
- Hold an **associate degree** or **≥ 60 semester credit hours** from an accredited institution (or both), **GPA ≥ 2.0**, unless specialty or exceptions in handbook **§1.2 E–F** apply.

**Issuance of a BEI certificate**

- Meet baseline, degree/credit, and **passing score** on the exam for the level sought (subject to **§1.2 E–F** grandfathering rules).

**Higher performance test — prerequisite certificates**

- Handbook **§1.2 Section G** defines which **BEI** and **RID** credentials qualify an applicant to sit for **Advanced**, **Master**, intermediary, and oral tracks (e.g., RID CSC, CI, CT, NIC tiers mapped to Advanced/Master). *See official table on the source page.*

## 2. Role & essential functions

**Standard role definition**

- A **BEI-certified interpreter** provides **sign language interpreter services** and must meet the **skills, experience, education**, and **other job-related requirements** of the position, including **essential job functions**.

*(Intermediary and specialized tracks have additional rules in other handbook chapters.)*

## 3. Physical competency standards (non-intermediary, non–Deaf interpreters)

The interpreter must be able to perform tasks including:

| Domain | Standard (summary) |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Hearing** | Hear, identify, and understand speech **without** relying on visual assistance. |
| **Speech** | Speak clearly enough for listeners to understand. |
| **Vision** | See hand shapes, movements, and facial detail at **3–6 feet**. |
| **Facial expression** | Control face muscles for brows, cheeks, mouth, nose. |
| **Manual dexterity** | Coordinated hand/arm movements as required for signing. |
| **Finger dexterity** | Precise finger movements of one or both hands. |
| **Wrist–finger speed** | Fast, simple, repeated finger/hand/wrist movements. |
| **Limb movement** | Place hands slightly above head; extend arms forward and to the sides. |
| **Limb movement speed** | Move arms quickly. |
| **Dual-limb coordination** | Coordinate both arms while sitting or standing. |
| **Head** | Nod and turn head side to side. |
| **Physical stamina** | Sustain **moderate** physical exertion for **≥ 30 minutes** without undue fatigue. |

## 4. Cognitive competency standards (non-intermediary, non–Deaf interpreters)

| Standard | Description (summary) |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Critical thinking** | Use logic and analysis on communication; adjust interpreting approach. |
| **Self-monitoring** | Monitor and assess interpretation during and after tasks. |
| **Selective attention** | Concentrate without distraction; sustain attention over time. |
| **Auditory attention** | Focus on one auditory source amid noise. |
| **Visual attention** | Focus on one visual source amid distracting movement. |
| **Mental stamina** | Sustain heavy cognitive load for **≥ 30 minutes** without breakdown. |
| **Working memory** | Hold concepts, words, numbers briefly while interpreting. |
| **Information ordering** | Track and order information correctly. |
| **Pattern inference** | Interpret partial or noisy information quickly. |
| **Time sharing** | Shift among tasks and information sources efficiently. |
| **Problem sensitivity** | Notice when something is wrong or likely to go wrong. |
| **Fluency of ideas** | Generate multiple ideas about a topic (quantity, not quality, per handbook). |
| **Breadth of knowledge** | Introductory-or-better familiarity across varied topics. |

## 5. Linguistic & cultural competency standards

**English (spoken)**

- Structure and content of English: meaning, spelling, composition, grammar.
- Understand ideas presented **orally**.
- Express ideas **orally** so others understand.

**Written English**

- Read and understand written material.
- Write so others understand.

**American Sign Language**

- Structure and content of ASL: lexical/phrasal meaning, grammar, articulation.
- Understand information via signs, gestures, classifiers, fingerspelling.
- Express information via the same.

**Culture**

- **In-depth** understanding of norms and mores of **American English-speaking** and **American Deaf** communities.

## 6. Professional conduct & workplace standards

**Interpersonal & conduct**

- **Social perceptiveness** — awareness of others’ reactions and reasons for them.
- **Independence** — workable approaches with minimal supervision.
- **Interpersonal relationships** — constructive, cooperative, sustained relationships.
- **Adaptability & flexibility** — tolerate variety and positive/negative change.
- **Emotional well-being** — emotional control for judgment and intellect.
- **Self-control** — composure; no inappropriate anger or aggression.
- **Professional decorum** — respect and professionalism in **all** interactions.
- **Problem solving** — identify problems, gather facts, draw valid conclusions.
- **Organizing, planning, prioritizing** — goals, plans, execution.
- **Conflict resolution** — resolve meaning/behavior conflicts.
- **Time management** — own time and respect others’ time.

## 7. Ethical standards (adopted Code of Professional Conduct)

Texas HHSC requires BEI-certified interpreters to follow the **Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf (RID) Code of Professional Conduct** (joint NAD–RID framework). Full text: **[RID.org](https://www.rid.org/)**.

**Seven tenets (as stated in handbook §1.3.1)**

1. Adhere to standards of **confidential communication**.
2. Possess the **professional skills and knowledge** required for the **specific** interpreting situation.
3. Conduct self in a manner **appropriate to the specific** interpreting situation.
4. Demonstrate **respect for consumers**.
5. Demonstrate **respect for colleagues, interns, and students** of the profession.
6. Maintain **ethical business practices**.
7. Engage in **professional development**.

## 8. Continuing education & recertification standards

**Five-year cycle — CEU totals**

- **10.0 CEUs** (100 clock hours) per five-year period, allocated as:
  - **6.0 CEUs** — interpreting-related topics  
  - **2.0 CEUs** — **ethics-related** topics  
  - **2.0 CEUs** — general studies  

- Court and trilingual certificates may have **additional** CEU rules (other chapters).

**Non–HHSC-approved ethics coursework**

- May count if the holder supplies: sufficient **course description**, recertification paperwork, and **professor letter** verifying **time by topic**.

## 9. Legal & regulatory compliance standards

- **Criminal history** — eligibility and ongoing discipline per handbook **Chapter 3** and applicable rules.
- **Expired certificate** — interpreting where a certificate is **required**, while holding **only** an **expired BEI** certificate, violates HHSC rules.
- **Testing integrity** — conduct on exams that constitutes **cheating** can result in denial, suspension, or revocation of certification.

---

## 10. Administrative & maintenance standards

- **Annual renewal** — pay renewal fee before expiration; update contact info on forms as required.
- **Name/address changes** — notify HHSC DHHS within **30 days** (documentation for legal name change).
- **Accommodations** — documented disability; request and documentation per **§1.4** and Civil Rights Office process.
